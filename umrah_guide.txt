# STEP 1 — IHRAM
**Step Type:** Multi-Substep (Manual Completion)
**Trigger:** User manually marks each substep complete
**Map Behavior:** No map tracking required. Map shows general direction toward Miqat.
**Bottom Sheet Behavior:** Sequential substep cards, each with instruction + dua + video

---

Ihram is the sacred state of ritual purity that a pilgrim must enter before performing Umrah or Hajj. It involves both a physical preparation — bathing, wearing the prescribed garments — and a spiritual commitment through the intention and the Talbiyah. The restrictions of Ihram (not cutting hair, not using perfume, not hunting, not engaging in marital relations) begin once the intention is made at or before the Miqat boundary. No pilgrim may skip this step or delay it past the Miqat.

---

### Substep 1.2 — Performing Ghusl (Ritual Bath)

**Instruction Text:**
Perform a complete ritual bath (Ghusl) before entering Ihram. This is a confirmed Sunnah of the Prophet (peace be upon him). It applies to men, women, and even women who are in their menstrual cycle — the ghusl is still recommended for them even though they will not perform Tawaf until their cycle ends. Use clean water and ensure the entire body is washed thoroughly.

**What the pilgrim says:**
The ghusl itself has no specific verbal dua. The pilgrim should begin with Bismillah and maintain an intention of purification for the sake of Ihram.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ

Transliteration:
Bismillah

English Meaning:
"In the name of Allah."

### Substep 1.3 — Wearing the Ihram Garments

**Instruction Text:**
For men: Wear two white seamless cloths. One wraps around the lower body (called the Izar) and one drapes over the upper body (called the Rida). The right shoulder should be left bare during Tawaf only — this is called Idtiba. Do not wear stitched clothes, underwear, socks that cover the ankles, or shoes that cover the heels. Do not apply perfume to the body or the Ihram cloth.

For women: Wear any modest, loose-fitting clothing that covers the entire body except the face and hands. Women should not wear niqab (face veil) or gloves while in Ihram. There is no specific color requirement for women.

**What the pilgrim says:**
No specific dua is prescribed for wearing the Ihram garments. However, upon completing the dressing, the pilgrim should make a general dua:

Arabic:
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أُرِيدُ أَنْ أَتَجَنَّبَ مَا نَهَيْتَ عَنْهُ وَأَنْ أَفْعَلَ مَا أَمَرْتَنِي بِهِ فَأَعِنِّي عَلَى ذَلِكَ

Transliteration:
Allahumma inni uridu an atajannaba ma nahayta 'anhu wa an af'ala ma amartani bihi fa a'inni 'ala dhalik

English Meaning:
"O Allah, I intend to avoid what You have forbidden and to do what You have commanded, so help me in that."

### Substep 1.5 — Reciting the Talbiyah (Entering Ihram State)

**Instruction Text:**
Immediately after the two rakah prayer, recite the Talbiyah aloud. This is the moment Ihram officially begins. Men should recite loudly. Women should recite quietly, audible only to themselves. Continue reciting Talbiyah throughout the journey — while walking, in transport, when ascending and descending, when meeting other pilgrims — until you begin Tawaf, at which point the Talbiyah stops.

**What the pilgrim says:**

Arabic:
لَبَّيْكَ اللَّهُمَّ لَبَّيْكَ، لَبَّيْكَ لَا شَرِيكَ لَكَ لَبَّيْكَ، إِنَّ الْحَمْدَ وَالنِّعْمَةَ لَكَ وَالْمُلْكَ، لَا شَرِيكَ لَكَ

Transliteration:
Labbayk Allahumma labbayk, labbayka la sharika laka labbayk, innal hamda wan-ni'mata laka wal-mulk, la sharika lak

English Meaning:
"Here I am, O Allah, here I am. Here I am, You have no partner, here I am. Verily all praise, grace, and sovereignty belong to You. You have no partner."

Arabic:

Transliteration:
Labbayk ilah al-haqq

English Meaning:
"Here I am, O God of Truth."

### Substep 1.6 — Ihram Restrictions Reminder Card

**Instruction Text:**
Now that you are in Ihram, the following are prohibited until you complete Taqsir/Halq at the end of Umrah:

- Cutting or trimming any hair from the body (head, beard, body hair)
- Cutting fingernails or toenails
- Applying perfume or scented products to the body or clothes
- Wearing stitched or fitted clothing (men only)
- Sexual intercourse or any intimate contact with a spouse
- Hunting or killing any land animal
- Proposing marriage or performing a marriage contract
- Covering the head intentionally (men only — accidental brief covering is forgiven)
- Wearing gloves or niqab (women only)

If any restriction is violated accidentally, the pilgrim should repent and may be required to offer a compensation (Fidyah) depending on the nature of the violation.

**What the pilgrim says:**
No specific dua — this is a reminder card only. The app should display this as an informational card, not a step requiring media.

# STEP 2 — MIQAT
**Step Type:** Checkpoint / Single Guided Step with Map Alert
**Trigger:** GPS proximity alert when approaching the Miqat boundary, plus manual confirmation

**Bottom Sheet Behavior:** Single instruction card with alert banner — "You are approaching the Miqat boundary. Ensure you are in Ihram before crossing."

---

Miqat refers to the specific geographic boundary lines defined by the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) around Makkah, within which no person intending Hajj or Umrah may cross without being in a state of Ihram. There are five Miqat points for pilgrims approaching from different directions. For pilgrims traveling by air — which is the majority today — the Miqat is typically crossed at 35,000 feet. Airlines announce this and some even display it on the flight map. Pilgrims who live inside the Miqat boundaries (including residents of Makkah) enter Ihram from their home. Pilgrims who live in Makkah itself perform Tawaf al-Qudum and enter Ihram for Sa'i from the Hil area (such as at Masjid al-Tan'eem, also called Masjid 'Aisha).

---

# STEP 3 — TAWAF
**Step Type:** GPS Tracked Step
**Trigger:** GPS tracking begins when user taps "Start Tawaf" after reaching Hajar al-Aswad radius
**Map Behavior:** Live map centered on Kaaba, showing user's circular path, lap counter, angular progress ring
**Bottom Sheet Behavior:** Lap counter (1/7), current dua card, manual +1 lap override button, distance to Hajar al-Aswad indicator before start

---

Tawaf is one of the three obligatory pillars of Umrah. It consists of seven complete circuits (called Shawts) around the Kaaba in a counter-clockwise direction, beginning and ending at the Black Stone (Hajar al-Aswad). It is performed on the ground floor of Masjid al-Haram (Mataf), on the first floor, or on the rooftop — all are valid, though ground floor proximity to the Kaaba is most preferred when accessible. The Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "Tawaf around the House is like prayer, except that Allah has permitted speech during it, so whoever speaks, let him speak only good." (Tirmidhi — Hasan Sahih)

There is no specific fixed dua prescribed for each of the seven circuits. The Sunnah is to make personal dua, recite Quran, and engage in dhikr. However, there is one confirmed supplication from the Sunnah said between the Yemeni Corner and the Black Stone in every circuit. The duas listed below for each lap are those used by the official Saudi Umrah app you shared — they are authentic Quranic supplications and Prophetic duas. They are optional and recommended, not mandatory.

---

# STEP 5 — SA'I
**Step Type:** GPS Tracked Step
**Trigger:** GPS tracking begins when user confirms they are at Safa and taps "Start Sa'i"
**Map Behavior:** Live map showing Safa-Marwa corridor, leg counter (1/7), distance to next endpoint, direction arrow, optional green marker zone indicator
**Bottom Sheet Behavior:** Leg counter, current location indicator (At Safa / Walking / At Marwa), dua card, manual +1 leg override button

---

Sa'i is the second obligatory pillar of Umrah. It commemorates the actions of Hajar (mother of Ismail, peace be upon him) who ran seven times between the hills of Safa and Marwa searching for water for her infant son. Allah immortalized her action in the Quran: "Indeed, Safa and Marwa are among the symbols of Allah. So whoever makes Hajj to the House or performs Umrah — there is no blame upon him for walking between them." (Quran 2:158) Sa'i consists of seven legs — the first leg goes from Safa to Marwa (Leg 1), the second from Marwa back to Safa (Leg 2), and so on. The seventh and final leg ends at Marwa.

An important Islamic clarification on counting: one complete trip from Safa to Marwa counts as one leg. A round trip (Safa → Marwa → Safa) counts as two legs. The total is seven legs, not seven round trips. The Sa'i ends at Marwa on leg 7.

Men should walk briskly (Harwala) between the two green fluorescent markers in the corridor — this is the Sunnah Ramal of Sa'i. Women walk normally throughout.

---

### Pre-Sa'i Substep 5.0 — Navigate to Mount Safa

**Instruction Text:**
After completing the two rakah at Maqam Ibrahim and drinking Zamzam, proceed to Mount Safa. As you approach Safa, recite the following verse — this is only said once, at the very start of Sa'i, never again in subsequent legs:

**What the pilgrim says (once only, approaching Safa for the first time):**

Arabic:
إِنَّ الصَّفَا وَالْمَرْوَةَ مِنْ شَعَائِرِ اللهِ فَمَنْ حَجَّ الْبَيْتَ أَوِ اعْتَمَرَ فَلَا جُنَاحَ عَلَيْهِ أَنْ يَطَّوَّفَ بِهِمَا

Transliteration:
Inna as-Safa wal-Marwata min sha'a'irillah. Faman hajja al-bayta aw i'tamara fala junaha 'alayhi an yattawwafa bihima

English Meaning:
"Indeed, Safa and Marwa are among the symbols of Allah. So whoever makes Hajj to the House or performs Umrah — there is no blame upon him for walking between them." (Quran 2:158)

Then say:
Arabic:
أَبْدَأُ بِمَا بَدَأَ اللهُ بِهِ

Transliteration:
Abda'u bima bada'allahu bih

English Meaning:
"I begin with what Allah began with."

# STEP 6 — TAQSIR / HALQ
**Step Type:** Multi-Substep (Manual Completion)
**Trigger:** User manually confirms completion
**Map Behavior:** No tracking needed
**Bottom Sheet Behavior:** Instruction card, dua, confirmation button "I have completed Taqsir/Halq"

---

Taqsir means shortening the hair from all over the head (for both men and women), while Halq means shaving the head completely (for men only). This act is obligatory and marks the exit from the state of Ihram. Without performing Taqsir or Halq, the pilgrim remains in Ihram and all restrictions remain in force. The Prophet (peace be upon him) supplicated three times for those who shaved their heads and once for those who shortened their hair, which indicates that Halq (full shave) is more virtuous for men. However, Taqsir is also completely valid.

A clarification many pilgrims are confused about: trimming just a few strands from one part of the head is NOT valid. The trim must cover the entire head — at least taking a fingertip-length from all around.

---

### Substep 6.1 — Understanding Taqsir vs Halq

**Instruction Text:**

For men: You may either shave the entire head (Halq — more virtuous) or trim hair from all parts of the head to at least a fingertip length (Taqsir). Do not trim only the front or sides — the trim must cover the whole head.

For women: Taqsir only — trimming approximately a fingertip's length (about one to two centimeters) from the ends of the hair. No shaving. Women typically trim a small bundle from the ends of their hair.

### Substep 6.2 — Performing Taqsir / Halq

**Instruction Text:**
Find a barber near Masjid al-Haram (for men) or perform Taqsir yourself or with help (for women, in a private setting). Before cutting, face the Qibla and make the following dua. Then proceed with cutting.

**What the pilgrim says:**

Arabic:
اللَّهُمَّ اغْفِرْ لِلْمُحَلِّقِينَ، اللَّهُمَّ اغْفِرْ لِلْمُحَلِّقِينَ، اللَّهُمَّ اغْفِرْ لِلْمُحَلِّقِينَ وَالْمُقَصِّرِينَ

"O Allah, forgive those who shave their heads. O Allah, forgive those who shave their heads. O Allah, forgive those who shave their heads and those who shorten their hair."
